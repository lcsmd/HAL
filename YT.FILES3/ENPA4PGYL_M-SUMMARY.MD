ONE SENTENCE SUMMARY:
Gordon Baker covers essential aspects of CC&Rs in HOAs, emphasizing their impact on Arizona residents and offering practical advice.

MAIN POINTS:
1. CC&Rs (Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions) are crucial for anyone living in an HOA community.
2. In Arizona, 56% of single-family homes had an HOA as of 2017, increasing to 80% for homes built since 2000.
3. It's possible to have CC&Rs attached to a property without an active HOA, affecting architectural and use restrictions.
4. Buyers should review CC&Rs during the title report process, with specific timelines for HOA notification and buyer disapproval rights.
5. Key areas of concern in CC&Rs include leasing and renting restrictions, parking and vehicle rules, and pet limitations.
6. Architectural restrictions often require committee approval for exterior changes to properties.
7. Residential use limitations may allow for home-based businesses under certain conditions.
8. Changing CC&Rs is extremely difficult, requiring a two-thirds majority vote among members.
9. Some CC&Rs may contain outdated or legally contradictory language, particularly regarding signage.
10. Reviewing Arizona's ARS Title 33, Chapter 16 is advised for understanding legal boundaries of HOAs.

TAKEAWAYS:
1. Understanding CC&Rs is essential for homeowners in HOA communities to avoid conflicts and ensure compliance.
2. Prospective buyers should thoroughly review CC&Rs and related documents before purchasing a property.
3. Key restrictions to look out for include those on leasing, parking, pets, architectural changes, and residential use.
4. The difficulty in changing CC&Rs underscores the importance of agreeing with them before buying into an HOA community.
5. Legal resources like Arizona's ARS Title 33 provide valuable guidance on the limits of HOA powers.
