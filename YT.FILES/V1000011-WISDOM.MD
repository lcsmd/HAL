SUMMARY:
A UK research group interviewed 12 AI experts using the Delphi method, revealing concerns about AI safety, national competition, quantum computing, misinformation, and tokenization of internet assets by 2040.

- AI safety may be compromised by 2040 due to competition between nations, not companies.
- Experts predict AI could cause mega-death events, with disagreements on the scale.
- Quantum computing's commercial relevance is not expected to significantly advance by 2040.
- AI's role in blurring truth from fiction could lead to an arms race in content verification.
- The future may see common ownership of internet assets via tokenship, not blockchain.
- Increasing software complexity makes distinguishing between accidents and manipulation challenging.
- Proposals for addressing AI risks include regulations, built-in safety checks, and better education.
- Experts suggest integrating social sciences to understand AI's societal impacts better.
- AI's potential to facilitate the spread of fake scientific papers and data is a concern.
- The reliance on scientific publications for fact-checking could be undermined by AI-generated misinformation.
