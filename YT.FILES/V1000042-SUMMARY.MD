ONE SENTENCE SUMMARY:
A detailed walkthrough of the essential apps and services used in a smart home setup, highlighting personal preferences and technical configurations.

MAIN POINTS:
1. Home Assistant is central to the smart home setup, with various apps and services enhancing functionality.
2. A dashboard app, Homer, organizes links to daily-used apps and services, inspired by a friend's setup.
3. The setup spans multiple physical locations, connected via site-to-site VPN for resource sharing and management ease.
4. Proxmox is used for virtualization, with a recent upgrade to version 8, replacing previous ESXi and vCenter setups.
5. TrueNAS (formerly FreeNAS) is preferred for storage, with TrueNAS Scale offering better Docker support and app integration.
6. OpenSense has replaced pfSense as the main firewall, offering frequent updates and comprehensive VPN support.
7. Home automation is managed through Home Assistant, an open-source platform for integrating smart devices.
8. Nextcloud provides a private alternative to cloud storage services like Google Drive, syncing files across devices.
9. Bitwarden is used for password management, with a transition from self-hosted to paid versions for security and convenience.
10. Transitioning from Plex to Jellyfin for media management due to user experience issues with Plex.

TAKEAWAYS:
1. A well-organized smart home setup can significantly enhance daily life through automation and efficient management.
2. Choosing the right software and hardware is crucial for creating a seamless and functional home automation system.
3. Open-source solutions like Home Assistant, TrueNAS, and Nextcloud offer powerful alternatives to commercial products.
4. Security and ease of access are prioritized through the use of VPNs, advanced firewalls, and password managers.
5. Continuous improvement and willingness to switch to better solutions are key to maintaining an optimal home lab environment.
