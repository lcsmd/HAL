Adam West, known for his role as Batman, navigated fame, personal struggles, and a diverse career, leaving a lasting legacy.

MAIN POINTS:
1. DC Comics' Batman initially used a gun, but this changed due to concerns over juvenile delinquency.
2. Frederick Wortham's book led to Batman becoming a non-lethal crime fighter.
3. Adam West's portrayal of Batman in the 1960s TV show revived the character with a comedic angle.

5. West's early life was marked by expulsion from schools and a move to Seattle after his parents' divorce.
6. After serving in the U.S. Army, West pursued acting, leading to his iconic role as Batman.
7. West's career suffered from typecasting after Batman, leading to struggles with roles and personal issues.
8. Despite challenges, West found new audiences through voice work in shows like "Family Guy."
9. Adam West's personal life included three marriages and struggles with depression and alcoholism.
10. West passed away in 2017 at 88, remembered fondly with a Bat-Signal tribute in Walla Walla.

TAKEAWAYS:
1. Adam West's Batman role significantly impacted his career, both elevating and limiting his opportunities.
2. Despite personal and professional challenges, West remained a beloved figure in pop culture.

4. The complexities of West's personal life contributed to his nuanced performances and public persona.
